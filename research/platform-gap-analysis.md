# PMP Study Platform — Competitive Gap Analysis (research, sourced)

> Compiled 2026-07-01 by a research agent. Every figure is source-attributed in the original.
> Several vendor pages (pmi.org, PrepCast store, pmstudycircle) returned HTTP 403 to automated
> fetches, so some numbers come from search extractions / third-party reviews — flagged in-line.
> Numbers move with exam updates (a PMBOK 8 update landed July 2026). "Bank size" is often
> marketing-inflated (full-exam Qs + quiz-pool Qs summed).

## The verified white space (our wedge)
**No researched platform advertises "work the entire large stack to completion without getting
stuck at the start."** The single loudest reason people abandon PMP prep is **overload + lack of
visible progress.** So the wedge is:

1. **Randomized-but-complete traversal** — shuffle the full stack once per attempt, keep a
   **persistent cursor**, resume where you left off, and march to the last unseen question.
   **Never restart at Q1.** (This is exactly the "stuck at Q20 of 212" pain.)
2. **Unambiguous done/not-done** — a visible "X of N answered · Y remaining" counter + persistent
   seen/unseen flag; a lifetime "% of bank worked through" number.
3. **Readiness vs. baseline** — a day-one diagnostic, then improvement over time as one indicator.
4. **Organize by the 3 ECO domains** (People 42 / Process 50 / Business 8) — familiar, matches the
   exam, enough. Optional filter by approach (Agile/Hybrid/Predictive). Avoid overwhelming taxonomies.
5. **Explanation on every question, always**, including why distractors are wrong.
6. **One clean study surface** ("sit down and just do questions" — Pocket Prep is the simplicity benchmark).
7. **Auto session persistence** (resume anywhere).

## What the best platforms do (patterns to copy)
1. ECO-domain organization with per-domain scoring (industry standard).
2. Weak-area / adaptive delivery (Study Hall adaptive engine; Pocket Prep "weak-area" + "missed" modes).
3. Explanations on every question — TIA's per-question **video** explanation is its most-praised feature.
4. A large **separate quiz pool** distinct from full mocks, unlimited repeats (PrepCast: 1,210 pool + 720 exam).
5. Realistic full **180 Q / 230 min** timed mocks + timing analytics.
6. **PMI mindset** content (situational reasoning > rote memorization).
7. **Focus over volume** — ~80% of analyzed r/pmp success stories used **ONE course (Ramdayal) + ONE simulator.**

## Why people abandon platforms (avoid these)
1. Resource overload / analysis paralysis (#1 driver).
2. No sense of progress (score-per-exam, no baseline, no "% complete").
3. Getting stuck / repeating the start of a big stack.
4. Question-quality mismatch (too easy = false confidence; too hard/wordy = discouragement).
5. Clunky/fragmented UX; jumping between a "course" and a separate "simulator."
6. Subscription friction (hard to cancel; unclear pass guarantee).
7. Time-limited access expiring before ready.

## Platform snapshot (verified figures; see original for full table + 26 sources)
- **PMI Study Hall (official):** adaptive engine by proficiency; 15 mini + 2 full exams (Essentials), more in Plus; total Q count not publicly verifiable; ~$49/$79. Strong analytics + adaptive weakness targeting.
- **PrepCast Simulator (Fichtner/Mplaza):** ~1,930 Qs (720 in 4 exams + 1,210 quiz pool; one page says "1,500+"); ECO-aligned; realistic, called harder/wordier than real exam; ~$139. Course layer criticized for **no progress tracking / no session saving.**
- **TIA / Andrew Ramdayal:** 1,100+ (simulator), 2,000+ (bundle); **video explanation on every question**; questions often called **easier** than real exam; ~$45. Domain analytics.
- **Pocket Prep:** 1,120 Qs + 1 mock; **phone-native, simplest**; quick/custom/weak-area/missed modes; category analytics; ~$21/mo–$125/yr.
- **Whizlabs:** ~1,000 Qs; dated UI, mixed quality; $15.95–$99/yr.
- **Velociteach InSite (Andy Crowe):** ~1,100 unique; polished, course-centric.
- **PMTraining:** 200+ samples + practice exams; ECO/2026-aligned; performance reports.
- **PrepAway:** braindump/dumps — **NDA/ethics risk, not recommended.**
- **Magoosh:** no verified live PMP product.

## Bottom line for our build
Randomized-complete-traversal engine + persistent cursor + plain "X done / Y left" + readiness-vs-baseline,
in a Pocket-Prep-simple single surface organized by the 3 ECO domains, with an explanation on every
question. Keep it ONE focused tool — do not add resources for their own sake (overload is the #1 killer).
