## Critical timing — we are on the seam
- **Exam on/before July 8, 2026** → uses the **January 2021 ECO** (aligned to PMBOK 6 + 7 + Agile Practice Guide). **← This is the learner's exam (July 8).**
- **Exam on/after July 9, 2026** → **new 2026 ECO**, aligned to **PMBOK 8** (40 processes), heavier agile/hybrid, Business Environment jumps to ~26%, new formats (case studies, drag-and-drop), ~185 Q / 240 min.

**We build for the January 2021 ECO.**

## The current (2021) exam structure — what it actually tests
- **Three performance domains (NOT the 49-process grid):**
  - **People — 42%** (14 tasks)
  - **Process — 50%** (17 tasks)
  - **Business Environment — 8%** (4 tasks)  → 35 tasks total.  *(PMI official: domain names + weights)*
- **~50% predictive / ~50% agile+hybrid, woven throughout all domains.** *(PMI official — direct ECO quote: "About half of the examination will represent predictive project management approaches and the other half will represent agile or hybrid approaches … not isolated to any particular domain or task.")*
- **180 questions / 230 minutes**, two optional 10-min breaks.
- **Predominantly situational** — prep/community estimates range "~50%" to "~80%+" scenario questions. *(Community/prep estimate — PMI publishes no official % situational.)*

## The #1 clarification (this drives our whole build)
**The exam is NOT organized by or tested on the 49 processes / ITTO memorization.** Since Jan 2021 it's re-anchored to the 3 ECO domains + 35 tasks; direct "which ITTO is an output of X" questions are rare (test-takers report **0–2**). ITTO *knowledge* still helps indirectly (~40–50% of items) because understanding process flow helps you answer "what should the PM do next?" *(PMI official structural change + community consensus.)*

**Honest guidance (reputable-provider consensus, incl. Andrew Ramdayal & Mike Griffiths):**
- Learn the 49-process framework enough to understand **flow and sequence** — use it as *scaffolding*.
- **Do NOT grind ITTO tables.**
- Spend the bulk of effort on **situational judgment, agile/hybrid mechanics, and the PMI mindset.**

## The PMI mindset (how you score situational questions) — prep-provider consensus
- **Assess before acting** — analyze / review / root-cause first; no impulsive action.
- **Don't escalate prematurely** — escalation is usually the wrong first move unless truly beyond the PM's control.
- **Servant leadership** — guide, support, empower the team; never micromanage or bottleneck.
- **Proactive communication**, prefer **face-to-face**; route changes through **formal change control**; engage stakeholders early.
- **Agile-first / empowered-team** default in ambiguous scenarios.
