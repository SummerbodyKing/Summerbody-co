## Part 2 — Directions, Tips & Pointers

### A. Language & word choice

- **Cut the five killer words — "actually," "well," "basically," "just," and ums/"you know."** [00:06:49] "These words are killer words — they literally kill the energy in the room… take a screenshot of that."
- **"How you do something is how you do everything."** [00:05:44] "How you show up on time, how you look on camera, how you treat people, how you strive for excellence."
- **WWFD — What Would Forbes Do.** [00:07:24] "She would clean up her languaging, she would be more intentional, and she would focus on serving others, not just telling your own story."
- **Open with "As someone who…" instead of a bare label.** [00:03:23, 00:37:40] "As someone who dealt with this and is now thriving — if you add that to the beginning of your pitch…"; demo: "As someone who grew up in foster care… I'm on a mission to help women find their voice."
- **Never use "and"-chained laundry lists when asked what you do.** [00:42:53] "If you use the word 'and' when someone asks you what you do, you've already bored them."
- **Less words, more impact.** [00:44:14] "Please take a screenshot — this is my favorite slide: less words, more impact."
- **Kill vague filler claims ("it changed my life," "amazing," "literally").** [02:06:06; 02:41:28] "'It changed my life' doesn't mean anything… what exactly, one thing, did it change that I could relate to?" McKenna: "'I just love the product, it's so amazing' — that quite literally means nothing."
- **Kill jargon — speak the customer's language.** [00:43:01; 01:51:49; 02:25:24] "'I'm an engineer of space dynamics' — huh?"; "My people don't care about proprioceptors — they care about how their ankles feel"; "even the word 'modality' makes me nuts."
- **Frame your numbers upward.** [02:18:11] "'Generate almost a million a year' — that sounds better than 'not quite a million.'"
- **Name what you're an expert IN — specificity or nothing.** [02:16:47] "You were an expert but you didn't say what you're an expert in… what are you an expert in?"
- **Watch every word — words carry weight.** [00:25:37; 02:43:44] "Start listening to the words that come out of your mouth… you say a lot of words but you don't really think about the impact on somebody else."

### B. The core pitching philosophy

- **Nobody cares what you do — they care what you can do for them.** [00:43:47] "The truth is that nobody cares what you do. We all care about what you can do for us." (Echoed at [00:36:54]: "That's not the question… here's the real question: what can you do for me?")
- **Say just enough to earn "tell me more."** [00:44:03] "You want that other person to go 'oh wow, tell me more' — not 'okay, that's nice.'"
- **Selling is not pitching: selling pushes, pitching pulls.** [00:44:31–00:45:35] "In selling you're pushing the product — quota, numbers, the car salesman… In pitching you're pulling people in, you solve a problem they feel deeply about." Pitching yields rapport, repeat business, and free referrals [00:45:13].
- **Get buy-in to *you* before the product.** [00:22:40; 00:53:48] Jake clip: "You weren't selling a product — people buy into you, because if they don't buy into you first, no matter what you sell them…"; "People buy from people they know, like and trust."
- **Never read your pitch — embody it.** [00:24:11; 01:54:54] "The show bombed because you cannot read your pitch, you have to embody a pitch"; to Al: "Never ever ever read what you're doing, because we don't feel your heart."
- **Say "you," not "I."** [00:24:38] "The big thing I coached him: stop saying the word I. I want you to hear how many times he says the word 'you' in the first 50 seconds" (Jack LaLanne clip).
- **Tell a story instead of listing specs** (the pen audition). [00:21:01] "Rather than say what the pen cost or that it had nice ink, I told a story… a pen like this can reach out and touch somebody's heart."
- **Don't lead with your trauma/survivor identity — reframe as your profession + mission.** [00:39:48–00:41:07] "The first thing you want to say is not that you're a stroke survivor — then you become a medical story… I would save that for later… it makes us look at you like 'oh poor her.'" Reframe: "I am a speaker, author, entrepreneur… of what it means to be a stroke survivor and how anybody can flourish."
- **Don't dump details until they're interested / until they ask.** [02:14:53–02:15:07; 02:04:07; 02:26:37] "They're not interested in details, because until they're interested they don't really care"; "You immediately launched into selling… nobody on this platform cares — they only care about themselves"; "I'll tell you what's in it when you ask me."
- **The Question Flip: open with the audience's pain, by show of hands.** [02:04:39; 02:00:49–02:02:05] "I wouldn't even say the word stem cell… I'd simply say: who here has suffered from joint pain, been to chiropractors and doctors and is still suffering?… the ones who raise their hands are the ones who say tell me more."
- **Find the real problem your customer suffers from — not the surface one.** [01:59:39–02:01:29] Insurance flip: "The real problem is they don't know if they have any income replacement plan if they get sick… You're not selling insurance, you are selling a lifetime of quality."
- **Use a vivid third-party story to make the pain real.** [02:02:17] "My friend Martin — 14 months in a nursing home, he had a stroke, he's broke… if he had you to save him."
- **Demonstrate before you explain** (the skincare demo). [02:25:43–02:26:40] "I don't tell you what it is, you're not even going to see the bottle. All I'm going to tell you is I am 66 years old — check this out… then and only then I go into [features]."
- **"You can be right or you can be rich."** [01:51:57–01:52:01] The insole owner wanted "proprioceptors" in the pitch; she refused, sold $20M. "When you're the creator of your own product, sometimes you like to talk too much about it."
- **A confused mind doesn't buy.** [00:35:47] "It doesn't buy into you, it doesn't buy product, it doesn't buy services — it just leaves."
- **Grab attention in the first 60 seconds; inspire an action.** [00:53:13–00:53:27] "A really powerful pitch grabs attention in the first 60 seconds… and inspires people to take an action. Life begins when people take an action."
- **Qualify yourself fast against your visible "discount."** [02:40:33–02:40:55] McKenna: "I look really young… so I very quickly have to qualify myself to be taken seriously. If you have a thick accent, identify where you're from… if you're not aware of that, it's costing you sales."
- **Own your identity — it's relatability, not a liability.** [02:09:16; 01:53:33] "Be a little more proud of being Mexican… 'as a very proud Mexican-American I help Latinos cracking the American market'"; "Sofía [Vergara] has such a hard-to-understand accent and is the highest-paid actress in America — stop telling us how small you want to play."
- **Turn weaknesses into positioning.** [02:09:53] "I may look young because I am young — and what's great about being young online… I'm going to introduce you to the world of AI."
- **If you use/invest in your own product, say so.** [02:32:58–02:33:15] "'As a professional I had a lot of choices — this is the product that I use, because I believe it is the best for you.'"
- **Anchor your numbers against what the listener knows.** [02:32:31–02:32:50] "Most 401ks historically earn 8%; we are offering 9.5% — oh, you just said that, I'd like to know more."
- **It's usually not the product, it's the explanation.** [02:31:51–02:32:05] "That's an annuity… it is not the product, it is the way that you are choosing to explain it" (after chat said "sounds like a scam").
- **Maybe the differentiator is YOU, not the product.** [02:34:43–02:35:16] "A lot of people offer [insurance], so maybe the big difference is not the product but it's you… your age, being a woman — leverage those realities."
- **End with a micro-close / call to action.** [02:06:38–02:19:52] "'If you want to know my secret, book a call with me'"; "'Want to know how? Book a call.'"
- **Everything is a pitch.** [00:13:31; 00:57:57] "Including getting your kids to clean their room, your spouse to take out the trash… pitching is you asking somebody to marry you."
- **Yeses are easy if you engineer them.** [01:58:54–01:59:18] "'Would you like to see something cool?' — I just got a hundred yeses… if you go in with a prejudice that it's hard, it will be hard."
- **You can't please everybody — don't try.** [01:58:37] "No matter what I do you cannot please everybody all the time, and that's too bad."
- **Know your level honestly: the "5" is the most dangerous pitcher.** [00:31:38–00:32:01] "Because you've sold something but you don't know how you did it — you can't recreate it."
- **Practice is the mechanism: "pitch practice makes profit."** [00:08:09; 00:23:07] "I did 1,500 products in five years… I practiced every single day."
- **Pitching is a learnable skill, not a gift.** [01:35:27; 01:50:13; 02:55:40] "Pitching is a skill, not something people are born with"; McKenna: "It is a skill set that anybody can learn… just like learning to install a fridge."
- **Closing is a system nobody taught you — learn the steps.** [01:48:31] "There is a systematic way of how to close a sale… if you never learn the steps you're always floundering."
- **The formula's 8 named steps** (her IP, only sketched): hub → discovery/question flip → pitch intro → assumption → relatability → springboard story → features/benefits ("febik") → call to action. [00:56:44–00:57:53; 01:29:20; 02:13:41 ("a combination of relatability and springboard story"); 02:26:12 ("febik, which is step number seven"); 02:25:39 ("relatability, that is step number five")]
- **Hope is not a strategy.** [00:02:43; 00:16:46] "You don't get to go out on camera and hope — hope is not a strategy; I use my formula to work every time."

### C. First impressions & presence (the "foundation")

- **The Three E's: be Excited, Engaging, Enrolled.** [00:32:38–00:33:54] "One, be excited — I'm tired of people boring me to tears. Two, engaging — does it feel like I'm talking to *you*?… Three, enrolled — if you want to get educated you need to enroll in programs."
- **Walk into every meeting with an intention.** [00:34:45] "When you go into a meeting, have an intention… my intention: I just want you guys to love me, not like me."
- **Visual alignment: look like what you say you do.** [00:35:31] "If somebody gets out of a Rolls-Royce in torn jeans… if it's not aligned, it's confusing — and a confused mind doesn't buy."
- **Mind the energy transfer.** [00:35:55] The third leg of her first-impression triad: implementing all three "builds trust faster, communicates clearer, attracts qualified leads."
- **Begin with the end in mind at every event.** [02:39:51] McKenna: "When I walk into a networking event I know I want to walk out with three new contacts — I know exactly why I'm there."

### D. On-camera / Zoom craft

- **Raise the camera — never let it look up your nose.** [01:17:41; 02:37:34] "Stacy, I'm looking up your nose… the camera's looking at you a little threateningly, I feel insecure"; "this little device makes you look like a rock star."
- **Remove visual distractions from frame.** [01:54:17] "See that fan above his head?… tilt your camera down, get the fan out, and we will like you more because we're not distracted."
- **Make your background telegraph your business.** [01:18:01] "Now you know just by looking at her that Stacy's a closet organizer and personal stylist — the clothes behind her let you know what she's up to."
- **Ditch generic virtual backgrounds.** [02:15:13] "Let's change your background — we don't want the Golden Gate Bridge… it doesn't instill a lot of confidence in you." (Her fix: a proper custom background, taught in the bonus training.)
- **Treat the camera as a gateway to one person.** [01:19:00] "Your camera is simply a gateway to a person sitting on the other side of it… I'm not talking to an entire audience, I'm talking to you."
- **Record what you do to multiply yourself.** [01:16:50–01:17:11] "If you can record what you do you can multiply your reach, make money faster, build connection and credibility instantly… turning browsers into buyers on autopilot."

### E. Mindset & self-talk

- **Stop labeling yourself ("I'm an introvert," "I can't").** [00:11:14; 01:52:04] "It breaks my heart — people playing small who literally say out of their mouths 'oh I'm an introvert'"; "Why did you label yourself? Technically I'm an introvert… I also understand I want to make a beautiful living and get out of my own way."
- **Ban the word "terrified" (and words like it) — your nervous system believes what you say.** [02:21:46–02:22:39] Repeat-after-me: "I am never going to say the word terrified again… nothing terrifies me. It can excite you, confuse you… when you said that, your whole nervous system agreed." "Every word that you say to yourself is how you show up to the world."
- **Be "outrageously delusional" — with self-love.** [02:22:43] "Look in that mirror and go, you know what kiddo, I love you… your heart is all that matters."
- **Believe you're made for more.** [00:19:33–00:19:54] "Maybe somebody put you down or bullied you, but you're like — wait, I'm made for more… is it okay if you tell yourself that you're special?"

I've spent the last 10 years becoming somebody that everybody wants to [meet]… let's get you to become that person worthy of them doing business with."
- **Get your ideas out before you die (the father's garage).** [00:28:36–00:29:03; 00:31:30] "If you don't get your ideas out to the world, don't expect someone else to do that… if you don't write your book while you're alive… when you die it's all over"; "If you die with that inside of you, shame on both of us."
- **Success breeds success and leaves clues; be an action taker.** [01:10:41–01:10:50] "Those of you who said yes right now — you're my kind of people… success breeds success and it leaves clues."
- **Positivity is contagious strategy.** [00:14:33] "The more we all elevate, that vibration extends to everybody else — we're gonna fix the negative people just by being super positive, also super profitable."
