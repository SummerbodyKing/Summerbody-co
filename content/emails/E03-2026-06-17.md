## BODY (FINAL, do not edit)

Hey [First Name],

9 days after I told you I wouldn't go quiet again, I went quiet. I owe you the truth, not an excuse.

On Mother's Day, my family lost someone we love.

The days after were the heaviest work I have ever done. I built his memorial page. I sat with the woman who knew him best and wrote his obituary line by line. I edited the funeral video. I designed every piece our family needed to send him home with the dignity he earned. Nobody volunteers for that work. I did it anyway because he was mine to honor.

The Campbell Family Reunion came nine days later. My proudest annual project. 85 of us from four bloodlines under one roof in Orlando. The family needed something to remember James by that was not only grief, so I poured into that too. We honored him Friday night at Ballroom A. We named him in the room. We kept living, because he would have hated for us to do anything else.

Then a new client found me from THIS email list, from the words I had already written you, and signed on. So I built for them too.

**I prayed for a cup of water. God gave me an ocean. I am grateful for every drop.**

Here is the part I need to be honest about.

The work I just described would have buried a younger version of me. Grief used to take a week of my life and ask for two more. This time it didn't, and the reason matters.

I made a decision. I would not let losing him cost me the people I am here to serve. I would not let grief cost me my business. **I would not let one more Black man disappear into his pain quietly.**

So I went and built a team. Not a team of headcount. A team of systems, partners, and tools that showed up when I called. Some of that team was human. Some was technology. All of it answered to my standard.

I will not pretend the technology did the work.

I wrote the obituary line by line with the woman who loved him most. I stood at the podium and read Venetia's thank-you to the room myself. I caught the wrong QR code on a poster at 2 AM before the printer ran 200 copies. I sat with my family for hours on a Sunday talking through who carries the next chapter when I cannot. The family decided Pastor Donald and Minister Vernell. The family. Not a machine.

What the technology gave me was time. **Time is the only thing grief tries to steal from you that you cannot get back any other way.**

It looked like me at 3 AM rewriting a name, catching a font that drifted, sending a draft back four times until it sounded like the man we lost. Fast hands at the keyboard. The heart, the eyes, the standard, and the final call on what was right, those stayed with me. They always will.

**The man at the funeral is the same man writing this email. Tired. Honest. Standing.**

It is June. National Men's Mental Health Awareness Month. So I will say the quiet part loud.

**Men disappear when life gets heavy. I disappeared once. I do not get to disappear again, because there are people behind me watching how I come back from this. You are one of them.**

The TEAM still stands. Together WE Achieve More. No man gets left behind, and that includes me.

I am happy. I am grateful. I am ambitious. I am excited. **And I am back.**
