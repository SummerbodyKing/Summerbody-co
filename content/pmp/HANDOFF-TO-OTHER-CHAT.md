## SECTION 5 — RISK DEEP DIVE

Risk is King's #1 chronic weak zone (42% baseline, barely moving). This section is critical.

### PIQQ ORDER (Four Risk Processes IN SEQUENCE)

1. **P — Plan Risk Management** = methodology doc (roles, categories, thresholds, tolerances)
2. **I — Identify Risks** = list them out
3. **Q — Qualitative Risk Analysis** = PRIORITIZE by probability × impact (subjective, fast)
4. **Q — Quantitative Risk Analysis** = NUMBERS on top-priority risks (Monte Carlo, Expected Monetary Value, expensive)

**Qualitative = PRIORITIZE. Quantitative = QUANTIFY.**

### RRAI — Documents That Update After Qualitative Risk Analysis

1. **R — Risk Register**
2. **R — Risk Report**
3. **A — Assumption Log**
4. **I — Issue Log**

King's trap: he picks only 2 of these. He must remember ALL 4.

### External Volatility → Risk Register

Financial markets, exchange rates, regulatory shifts, supplier volatility, political/economic conditions = update the RISK REGISTER. Not Stakeholder Register. Not Marketplace Conditions. Not Lessons Learned.

### Four Risk Response Strategies

- **AVOID** = eliminate the risk source or don't do the project
- **TRANSFER** = shift to third party (insurance, bonds, warranties, contracts) — has a premium cost
- **MITIGATE** = reduce probability OR reduce impact (proactive planning)
- **ACCEPT** = passive (do nothing) or active (contingency reserves)

### Reserves

- **Contingency Reserve** = known unknowns. Belongs IN the cost baseline.
- **Management Reserve** = unknown unknowns. NOT in the cost baseline.

### Risk Techniques (King keeps confusing these)

- **Delphi Technique** = anonymous polling, iterative. IDENTIFY risks. Great for remote/distributed teams.
- **Monte Carlo Analysis** = quantitative simulation using probability distributions. ANALYZE cost/schedule.
- **Decision Tree Analysis** = quantitative, probability of occurrence. ANALYZE.
- **Affinity Diagram** = qualitative, organize by categories. ANALYZE.

Only Delphi is for IDENTIFICATION. The others are ANALYSIS.

## SECTION 7 — HYBRID META-RULE

On Hybrid questions, King's brain reaches for generic "good PM" answers (servant leader, rapport, mapping). PMI wants the specific answer to the specific question asked.

**Exam day rule for Hybrid questions:**

1. Read the question TWICE.
2. Ask: "Is PMI asking for a UNIVERSAL answer or a SITUATIONAL one?"
3. If the question TELLS you the PM's orientation, situation, or context — match your answer to THAT specific context.
4. If the question is about a MUST-HAVE for ALL PMs — pick the universal answer (honesty, transparency, ethics).

**Traps King fell for:**
- Picked "servant leader" when the question asked what a PM MUST have (universal = honesty)
- Picked "build rapport" when the PM was described as DIRECTIVE (match orientation)
- Picked "stakeholder mapping" when the ask was to BUILD relationships (mapping = prep)

## SECTION 8 — WHEN YOU DON'T KNOW: DECISION TREE

1. **Eliminate the two "extreme" answers.**
   - "Immediately halt" = wrong (overreaction)
   - "Ignore it" = wrong (irresponsibility)
   - "Never permitted" = wrong (too rigid)

2. **Of the two remaining, pick the more "PM-like":**
   - The one that follows PROCESS beats the one that skips it.
   - The one that involves the RIGHT STAKEHOLDER beats the one that goes over their head.
   - The one with FEWER assumptions beats the one requiring you to invent context.

3. **Check for keyword traps:**
   - "FIRST" = earliest legitimate step, not most action-y sounding
   - "BEST" = solves root cause, not symptom
   - "MOST APPROPRIATE" = follows PMI's ethical/process framework

4. **Agile vs Predictive:**
   - Agile keywords: iteration, sprint, user story, product owner
   - Predictive keywords: baseline, phase, CCB, change request

5. **When two answers seem equally good:**
   - Pick the one involving Sponsor or Team, not HR/Legal/"documents it."
   - PMI rewards HUMAN CONNECTION over PAPERWORK.
