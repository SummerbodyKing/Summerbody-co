## SECTION 2 — THE 10 LOCKED MNEMONICS

Each one is sourced to a specific PMI Study Hall question. If you get a question that smells like this pattern — this is the answer.

### 1. "POC BEFORE CCR"
**Proof of Concept before Change Request.**
When someone requests a change (predictive or otherwise), you assess the impact FIRST, THEN submit the formal change request WITH the analysis attached. Never raise a change request without data.

**Sources:** Mini Exam 13 Q8 (environmental materials), Your PMP CMD app "external regulatory change" question. Both PMI solutions confirm.

### 2. "REGISTER LISTS. PLAN PROCESSES. MATRIX MEASURES."
Three stakeholder documents. Three different jobs.
- **Stakeholder REGISTER** = the list of who's involved
- **Stakeholder ENGAGEMENT PLAN** = the process for managing their expectations
- **Stakeholder ASSESSMENT MATRIX** = a tool for measuring current vs desired engagement

**Sources:** Mini Exam 14 Q2 (multinational, align expectations → Engagement Plan). PMI solution confirmed.

### 3. "AUDIENCE CHANGES = COMMS PLAN UPDATES"
When the WHO you're communicating with changes → update the Communications Management Plan.

**Sources:** Mini Exam 14 Q1 (steering committee replaced by single sponsor). PMI solution confirmed.

### 4. "COMPLAINTS OR DECLINED MEETINGS = COMMS PLAN"
When stakeholders are unhappy about communication or skipping meetings, the ROOT CAUSE is usually a missing/weak Communications Management Plan.

**Sources:** Mini Exam 1 Q4 (stakeholders complaining, declining meetings → Comms Plan). PMI solution confirmed.

### 5. "RISK FIRST. MOTIVATE THE MARATHON."
In Agile: high-risk stories go in EARLY sprints, not saved for later. Motivation is ONGOING (benefits system, servant leadership), not a party at Go-Live.

**Sources:** Mini Exam 14 Q4 + Mini Exam 1 Q2 (both: high-value/high-risk stories in early iterations). Mini Exam 14 Q5 (motivation = benefits system, not end-of-project celebration). PMI solutions confirmed.

### 6. "CAN'T CONTROL IT? ADJUST MY PLAN."
When someone else (supplier, functional manager, external partner) causes a delay — you do NOT ask them to move faster. You adjust YOUR timeline. Do not bypass required work.

**Sources:** Mini Exam 14 Q3 (server delayed) + Mini Exam 1 Q1 (server won't support testing, wait then reevaluate). Both PMI solutions confirmed. You've missed this THREE TIMES. Do not miss it on the exam.

### 7. "PMO ACROSS. SPONSOR ONE PROJECT. PM EXECUTES."
- **PMO** = enterprise-wide, integral stakeholder, key decision-maker across ALL projects
- **SPONSOR** = champions and funds ONE specific project
- **PM** = executes; not the integral stakeholder
- **CUSTOMER** = external, uses the output

**Sources:** Mini Exam 14 Q7 (authority across the life of each project → PMO, per PMBOK 6). PMI solution confirmed.

### 8. "COMPLY + EXPLAIN. ONE-ON-ONE. STEERING COMMITTEE LATER."
When a stakeholder resists a compliance/regulatory requirement, the FIRST move is a direct 1-on-1 conversation with them to explain the need. Never exclude, never sneak, never jump to a steering committee first.

**Sources:** Mini Exam 14 Q6 (customer resists compliance audit). PMI solution confirmed.

### 9. "PURPOSE BEFORE PEOPLE. PURPOSE BEFORE ANALYSIS."
The FIRST step on any new project or business initiative is to define vision/mission/purpose. THEN identify stakeholders. THEN resources. THEN detailed analysis. Never skip ahead to schedule, impact analysis, or benchmarking.

**Sources:** Mini Exam 13 Q9 (budget/time constraints → meet sponsor for purpose FIRST) + Mini Exam 1 Q3 (R&D new line → vision/mission session before impact analysis). Both PMI solutions confirmed.

### 10. "TAILOR BEFORE BUILD. USE WHAT EXISTS FIRST." **(NEW)**
When starting a new project in an org with existing assets/templates/OPAs — adapt and tailor what exists FIRST. Don't jump to procurement, WBS, or sign-off before leveraging OPAs.

**Sources:** Mini Exam 1 Q5 (business transformation, before defining scope → tailor existing assets). PMI solution confirmed.

### BONUS MICRO-MNEMONIC: "MATCH DOC TO ASK"
Sponsor asks about RESOURCES → RBS (Resource Breakdown Structure)
Sponsor asks about SCOPE → SOW (Statement of Work)
Sponsor asks about BUDGET → BES (Budget Estimate Sheet)
Sponsor asks about WORK → WBS (Work Breakdown Structure)

#### 16. "MANAGER CONTROLS PROCESSES. LEADER INSPIRES PEOPLE."
Manager = authority, control, efficiency, effectiveness, planning, organizing, controlling resources, focus on objectives.
Leader = inspire, motivate, set vision, align people, drive momentum.

**Trap you fell for:** Picking "leader" (B) when the question described control/efficiency/authority. Those are MANAGER traits, not leader traits.

**Source:** Mini Exam 3 Q5 (manager vs leader assessment). PMI solution confirmed.

#### 17. "NO HISTORICAL DATA? BUILD THE RISK PLAN YOURSELF."
When historical data is unavailable, the PM must PROACTIVELY develop the risk management plan from scratch AND inform stakeholders. Just telling the sponsor and continuing isn't enough.

**Trap you fell for:** Picking "inform sponsor and continue" (C) with HIGH confidence. PMI wants proactive planning, not reactive informing.

**Source:** Mini Exam 3 Q1 (critical project, no historical data). PMI solution confirmed.

#### 18. "PLAN PREVENTS. AUDIT DETECTS. UPDATE FIXES."
When something goes wrong (like using an old template that fails audit), the PREVENTIVE root cause fix is having a proper Risk Management PLAN that includes monitoring/updating built in.
- Internal audit BEFORE external audit → detection, not prevention
- Updating the template after the fact → reactive fix, not prevention
- Blaming the org for providing the template → not owning it

**Source:** Mini Exam 3 Q4 (old risk register template, external audit NC). PMI solution confirmed.

#### 19. "INSURANCE = TRANSFER. NOT JUST RECORDING."
Risk Response Strategies — memorize all four for the exam:
- **AVOID** = don't do the project OR eliminate the risk source
- **TRANSFER** = shift to third party (INSURANCE, performance bonds, warranties, contracts) — has a premium cost
- **MITIGATE** = reduce probability OR reduce impact (proactive planning)
- **ACCEPT** = passive (do nothing) or active (contingency reserves)

When facing a significant financial penalty risk with a medium threshold, insurance = TRANSFER is the strategy.

**Trap you fell for:** Picking "record it in risk register and review it" (D). That's not a strategy. That's a book-keeping action. Register it AND choose a response strategy (in this case: transfer via insurance).

**Source:** Mini Exam 3 Q3 (bidding penalty clause, medium risk threshold). PMI solution confirmed.

#### 20. "DELPHI = IDENTIFY (REMOTE). MONTE CARLO/DECISION TREE = QUANTITATIVE ANALYSIS. AFFINITY = QUALITATIVE ORGANIZE."
Four risk techniques you keep confusing. Trigger words:

- **DELPHI TECHNIQUE** → anonymous polling, iterative consensus, GREAT for distributed/remote teams that can't meet in person. This is a RISK IDENTIFICATION tool.
- **MONTE CARLO ANALYSIS** → quantitative simulation, cost/schedule iterations, uses probability distributions. RISK ANALYSIS.
- **DECISION TREE ANALYSIS** → quantitative, probability of occurrence. RISK ANALYSIS.
- **AFFINITY DIAGRAM** → qualitative, organizes ideas by categories/similarities. RISK ANALYSIS.

**Trap you fell for:** Picking Monte Carlo (D) for a "team in 6 countries need to identify risks" question. Monte Carlo is ANALYSIS not IDENTIFICATION. Delphi is the correct identification method for distributed teams.

**Source:** Mini Exam 3 Q2 (team in 6 countries, best risk identification method). PMI solution confirmed.

#### THE 4 RISK MANAGEMENT PROCESSES — IN ORDER (from Mini Exam 2 Q4)

**"PIQQ"** — memorize this order.

1. **P — Plan Risk Management** = create the risk management plan (methodology, roles, categories)
2. **I — Identify Risks** = list them out
3. **Q — Qualitative Risk Analysis** = **PRIORITIZE by probability and impact** (subjective, fast)
4. **Q — Quantitative Risk Analysis** = **NUMBERS on the top-priority risks** (Monte Carlo, EMV, expensive)

**Trap you fell for:** picking "Plan Risk Management" when the question asked about PRIORITIZING. Plan Risk Management is the METHODOLOGY doc, not the prioritization act. Qualitative Risk Analysis is prioritization.

**Mnemonic:** **"Qualitative = PRIORITIZE. Quantitative = QUANTIFY."**

#### AFTER QUALITATIVE RISK ANALYSIS — 4 DOCS UPDATE (from Mini Exam 2 Q3)

**"RRAI"** — Risk Register, Risk Report, Assumption Log, Issue Log.

1. **Risk Register** = individual risks + their new priority
2. **Risk Report** = overall project risk exposure
3. **Assumption Log** = assumptions validated or invalidated during analysis
4. **Issue Log** = risks that became actual issues

**Trap you fell for:** picking "Risk Register + Risk Report" only. INCOMPLETE. You also need Assumption Log and Issue Log.

**Mnemonic:** **"After Qualitative → RRAI updates."**

## SECTION 4 — WHEN YOU DON'T KNOW: DECISION TREE

When a question feels unclear, walk through these in order:

1. **Eliminate the two "extreme" answers.**
   - "Immediately halt" — usually wrong (overreaction)
   - "Ignore it" — usually wrong (irresponsibility)
   - "Never permitted" — usually wrong (too rigid)

2. **Of the two remaining, pick the more "PM-like":**
   - The one that follows PROCESS beats the one that skips it.
   - The one that involves the RIGHT STAKEHOLDER beats the one that goes over their head.
   - The one with FEWER assumptions beats the one requiring you to invent context.

3. **Check for keyword traps:**
   - "FIRST" = the earliest legitimate step, not the most action-y sounding
   - "BEST" = the one that solves the root cause, not the symptom
   - "MOST APPROPRIATE" = the one that follows PMI's ethical/process framework

4. **When agile vs predictive is unclear:**
   - If the question mentions "iteration," "sprint," "user story," "product owner" → Agile.
   - If it mentions "baseline," "phase," "CCB," "change request" → Predictive.
   - If both — hybrid — the answer usually leans toward whichever methodology the current PHASE is in.

5. **When two answers seem equally good:**
   - Pick the one that involves the SPONSOR or the TEAM, not the one that goes to HR or Legal or "documents it."
   - PMI rewards HUMAN CONNECTION over PAPERWORK.
